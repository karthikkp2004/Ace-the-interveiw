Number Series

Here you'll find various techniques to solve number series questions in placements, along with tips, formulas, and tricks.

What is a Number Series?

A number series is a sequence of numbers that follow a particular pattern or rule. In placement exams, questions based on number series are quite common. The goal is to find the missing number in a given series based on a certain pattern.

Common Patterns in Number Series:

  • Arithmetic Progression (AP): In this type of series, the difference between consecutive numbers is constant. For example, 2, 5, 8, 11, ... (difference is +3).
  • Geometric Progression (GP): In a geometric series, each term is found by multiplying the previous term by a constant number. For example, 3, 6, 12, 24, ... (multiplying by 2).
  • Square/Cube Series: Sometimes, the numbers in the series are squares or cubes of consecutive numbers. For example, 1, 4, 9, 16, ... (squares of 1, 2, 3, 4, ...).
  • Fibonacci Series: A Fibonacci series is a sequence where each number is the sum of the two preceding ones. For example, 0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, ...
  • Odd/Even Number Series: The series may consist of only odd or even numbers, such as 1, 3, 5, 7, ... or 2, 4, 6, 8, ...

Tips for Solving Number Series Questions:

  • Look for a consistent difference or ratio between the numbers in the series.
  • Try to recognize the pattern or sequence, such as squares, cubes, prime numbers, etc.
  • Don't hesitate to check for alternative patterns, like alternating operations or factorials.
  • Sometimes the missing number might be the sum or difference of certain terms.

By practicing various types of number series questions, you can improve your problem-solving skills and perform better in placement exams.
